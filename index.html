Abstract

We study how the optimal trajectories of the penalty parameter μk and denoiser noise level σk — the core hyperparameters of HQS-based Plug-and-Play restoration — change with the blur kernel. Existing methods apply one hand-crafted schedule to every input. We instead freeze a pretrained DRUNet entirely and expose only the 24 schedule scalars (μ and σ over 12 iterations) as learnable parameters, trained end-to-end by differentiable unrolling, independently for each of 12 kernels. Because the denoiser never changes, every performance difference is attributable to the schedule alone. The learned trajectories diverge from the hand-crafted baseline into patterns governed by the kernel's shape (Gaussian vs. non-Gaussian), scale, and the topology of the motion path (open- vs. closed-loop), and yield an average +0.12 dB on Set3C — evidence that the HQS schedule is an interpretable, actionable degree of freedom, not a fixed convention.

Key findings

01

The schedule is a lever, isolated

Freezing DRUNet removes representational capacity as a confound. Any gain comes purely from the (μ, σ) schedule — measured, not assumed.

02

Structure → trajectory

Gaussian-small tracks the baseline; Gaussian-large holds σ high early; open-loop motion invents a non-monotonic dip-then-plateau. The kernel's geometry is legible in the schedule.

03

Divergence predicts gain

The kernels whose learned σ departs most from the baseline — Gaussian-large and open-loop — also gain the most PSNR. The headroom lives where the one-size schedule fits worst.

What the open-loop kernel does

For an open-loop kernel (energy spread along a curved, non-closing path), the optimizer keeps the prior strong through the middle iterations: both σ and μ rise above the baseline together rather than trading off. The cost is temporary — intermediate iterations sit below the baseline in PSNR — but the final reconstruction wins, because the strong mid-stage prior suppresses structured artifacts that would be hard to remove later. A useful caution falls out of this: intermediate-iteration quality is not a proxy for final quality when comparing PnP-HQS schedules.
